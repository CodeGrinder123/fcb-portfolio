About Taguchi

Taguchi established an approach for using statistics to improve the quality of manufactured items starting in the 1950s. Some traditional Western statisticians have criticized Taguchi's approach, but others have acknowledged many of the notions he offered as genuine additions to the body of knowledge.

Genichi Taguchi

(January 1, 1924 – June 2, 2012)


The originator of the famed Taguchi Methods also known as Robust Design, which have profoundly influenced product development, engineering and the global quality movement. Taguchi worked with quality pioneer W. Edwards Deming to help Japanese companies set the bar for quality and Japan’s post World War II ascent, to help transform Japan from also-ran to global leader. He authored more than 40 books and received numerous awards for his contributions to Quality Engineering.

Biography

The place he was born was a place that is known for clothing industry and their family has a Kimono business.He studied textile engineering at Kiryu Technical College. Taguchi was drafted to the Astronomical Department of the Navigation Institute of the Imperial Japanese Navy. He work at the Statistical Mathematics of the Ministry of Education and further develop his statistical analysis skill.Taguchi also obtained Ph.D. in Statistic and Mathematics Science at the Kyushu University in 1962 and taught at Aoyama Gakuin University in 1965.
